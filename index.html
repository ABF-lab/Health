💡 Program Objective & Hackathon Strategy

1. India's Primary Health Threat

India is recognized as the diabetes and hypertension capital of the world. Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) are India’s primary health threat, accounting for roughly 60% to 63% of all deaths in the country, with cardiovascular diseases alone causing 32.1% of these fatalities. Diabetes and Hypertension are high metabolic risk factors impacting tens of millions, particularly concentrated and rising rapidly in urban and poverty-stricken brackets.

2. Economic & Social Impact on Urban Poor

The economic impact of NCDs on communities in urban poverty-stricken brackets is devastating, frequently causing catastrophic health expenditures and severe household impoverishment. Because NCDs are chronic and require lifelong care, they trap low-income urban families in a cycle of debt and multi-generational poverty. When an individual's health worsens, the social and economic burden inevitably cascades back onto the community itself through shared charity and loss of productivity.

3. The Solution: Hyper-Local Preventive Screening at Mass Scale

Instead of waiting for patients to visit distant hospitals, ABF brings mass-scale diagnostics directly to the community level. We leverage existing trusted networks of mosque committees, various jamaats, and local NGOs to establish screening hubs directly at mosques. Staffed by trained local volunteers and masjid staff, the hubs provide regular, proactive diagnostics completely free of cost.

4. Durable Equipment & Low Recurring Costs

Each screening point is equipped with a kit consisting of a digital BP apparatus, a Glucometer with lancets/strips, a weighing scale, and a Stadiometer along with a training manual & reference booklet, costing around ₹5,000 (approx).

  • Durable Capital: The hardware kit is durable and easily lasts for at least a couple of years.
  • Ultra-Low Running Costs: At mass scale, the recurring cost for testing each patient is only **₹12 to ₹15** for consumables (batteries, gluco testing strips, lancets, alcohol swabs, etc.).

5. Strategic Scaling Roadmap

  • Phase A (Technology Build): Build smart, intuitive apps, data capture utilities, and dashboards optimized for low-end Android mobile devices with offline-first capabilities.
  • Phase B (Hyper-Local Pilot): Launch the pilot phase across 100 pre-identified mosques and community centers in Bangalore.
  • Phase C (Regional Scaling): Scale up operations across Bangalore $\rightarrow$ Karnataka state $\rightarrow$ National scale across India.

6. Financial Sustainability & Monetization Models

  • Cross-Subsidization (Freemium Model): Offer the exact same screening services to middle-class and upper-middle-class individuals for a nominal fee of **₹50 per screening**, cross-subsidizing and keeping it completely free for whoever cannot afford it.
  • Branding & Marketing Sponsorships: Partner with health-conscious brands and medical services for targeted marketing, wellness sponsorships, and co-branded health awareness campaigns.
